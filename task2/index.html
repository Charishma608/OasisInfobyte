MOTHER TERESA

ABOUT THE GREAT LADY...

Mother Teresa, often referred to as the "Saint of the Gutters," was a remarkable humanitarian
and a symbol of compassion. Born in Skopje, North Macedonia, in 1910, she dedicated her
life to serving the poorest of the poor in the slums of Calcutta, India. Her journey of selflessness and love for
humanity began as a young nun, but it soon led her to establish the Missionaries of Charity, an organization
that provided care, shelter, and hope to those abandoned by society. Mother Teresa's unwavering commitment to alleviating
suffering and promoting peace earned her numerous accolades, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. Her legacy
lives on as an inspiration to countless individuals around the world.

Throughout her life, Mother Teresa exemplified the essence of kindness and humility. She believed
that every person, regardless of their background or circumstances, deserved love and respect. Mother Teresa's
tireless work in the slums and streets of Calcutta left an indelible mark on the world,
reminding us all of the profound impact one person can make through acts of love and service.
Her legacy continues to shine brightly, and her words, "Not all of us can do great things,
but we can do small things with great love," serve as a timeless reminder of the power of compassion
and the boundless potential for positive change that exists within each of us.
